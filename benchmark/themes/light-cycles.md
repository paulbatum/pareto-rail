# Light Cycles

Build a level where you ride a light cycle across an endless circuit floor, hunting the rival riders loose on it. Every cycle drags a solid wall of light behind it, and those walls are the world: they stack up as the fight goes on until you are threading a maze the duel itself built. Cut the run into duels — each rival you close on breaks away into a fast sequence of right-angle cuts, drifts, and stacked S-curves before you finally take it.

Go bright and flat, like a two-ink print. No sky, no haze, no depth fade: a flat cream void, a hard horizon, everything solid shapes in two colors with visible halftone in the fills. Yours is a warm red, the rivals a deep teal, and where the two ribbon walls cross the inks combine into a third color — use that overlap as the level's own visual language. Score it with orchestra and analog synthesizer treated as one instrument: heavy low brass and massed strings locked to a four-on-the-floor pulse, arpeggiated sequencer lines underneath, all of it in the same wide cinematic space. Monumental and danceable at once, gaining a layer with each rival you put down.

Enemies come from the riders. A rival doesn't fight you directly at first — it calls down flights of fast aerial drones that peel off it in formation, so killing the rider is what stops the stream.

Finale: the last rival runs you into a gauntlet of shutter barriers dropping across the track, each fed by a gate system off to the side. Shoot the gate in time and the barrier folds away as you reach it; miss the window and you take the wall. Clear the last one and the rival has nowhere left to run — its ribbon lifts off the plain and the teal drains out of the world, leaving the circuit printed in your red alone, level ends.
