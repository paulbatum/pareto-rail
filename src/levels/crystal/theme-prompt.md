# Crystal Corridor — baseline theme prompt

_Written retroactively against the finished level, as a calibration reference for
benchmark theme authoring. The target level of detail: two independent builds from
this prompt should be recognizable as siblings, while still differing clearly in
artistry. The prompt below is written the way a person would type it into an
arena-style comparison box, so it stays light on technical detail._

---

I want a short on-rails shooter that plays like Rez: you fly forward automatically
through a tunnel, hold to charge, sweep the cursor across enemies to chain lock-ons,
release to fire the whole volley. The theme is a neon crystal corridor.

**The world.** You're flying down a winding tunnel deep inside some enormous crystal
formation. Everything is glowing wireframe on near-black — thin hot lines, no solid
surfaces — mostly cyan and magenta. The tunnel is receding wireframe ribs, with
faceted crystal clusters drifting past in the dark outside. Cold, clean, precise.

**Gameplay.** Short and dense, around 45 seconds, built to be replayed. Three acts.
Act one is a warm-up: calm formations of crystal shapes that hold, drift, or orbit,
so you learn the sweep-and-release rhythm. In act two the corridor wakes up: waves
get denser and a nastier crystal type appears that shoots glowing shard bolts back
at you — they home in on your view, and you have to shoot them down before they
land. You can only take three hits. Then a short breath of quiet, and act three is
a boss: a huge crystal lattice filling the tunnel ahead, with orbiting shield
plates you break away in stages to expose the core. Killing it is the finale.

**Music and sound.** All electronic, minor key, mid-120s BPM, arranged in three
acts that escalate with the action. The crucial part is the synesthesia: every
player sound snaps to the beat grid, and each kill plays the next note of a hidden
melody, so chaining a volley of kills out of a dense wave performs an actual
melodic run. Locks tick upward in pitch as you paint targets. Give each act its own
instrument voices so the level audibly changes character as it goes, and make the
boss act toll — shield breaks and core hits landing like heavy bells.

**Details that sell it.** Locking a crystal flares it magenta. Kills shatter into
shard bursts. The boss entrance should break the palette for a moment — flood the
corridor in hot gold so you feel something bigger has arrived. Even the START and
REPLAY letters should be built in the crystal language: little cell-grid glyphs
with glowing edges that you shoot to begin.
