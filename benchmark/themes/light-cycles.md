# Light Cycles

Build a level where you ride a light cycle at absurd speed across an endless circuit floor, hunting the rival riders loose on it. Every cycle drags a solid wall of light behind it, and those walls are the world: they stack up across the plain as the duel goes on until you are threading a maze the fight itself built. Speed is the point — the grid tears past underneath, and the rail whips through hard right-angle cuts, drifts, and stacked S-curves as you and the rivals trade the lead. Cut the run into duels: each rival you close on breaks away into a fast sequence of turns before you finally take it.

Go bright and flat, like a two-ink print. No sky, no haze, no depth fade — a flat cream void, a hard horizon line, and everything rendered as solid shapes in two colors with visible halftone in the fills. Yours is a warm red, the rivals a deep teal, and where the two ribbon walls cross, the inks combine into a third color; use that overlap as the level's own visual language. The score is orchestra and analog synthesizer treated as one instrument: heavy low brass and massed strings locked to a four-on-the-floor pulse, arpeggiated sequencer lines running underneath, everything sitting in the same wide, cinematic space. It should feel monumental and danceable at once, and it should gain a layer with each rival you put down.

Enemies come from the riders. A rival rider doesn't fight you directly at first — it calls down flights of fast aerial drones that peel off it in formation, so killing the rider is what stops the stream.

Finale: the last rival runs you into a gauntlet of shutter barriers dropping across the track, each one fed by a gate system off to the side. Shoot the gates in time and the barrier folds away as you reach it; miss the window and you take the wall. Clear the last gate and the rival has nowhere left to run — its ribbon lifts off the plain and the teal drains out of the world, leaving the whole circuit printed in your red alone, level ends.
