# Light Cycles

Build a level where you ride a light cycle at absurd speed across an endless circuit floor, hunting the rival riders loose on it. Every cycle drags a solid wall of light behind it, and those walls are the world: they stack up across the plain as the duel goes on until you are threading a maze the fight itself built. Speed is the point — the grid tears past underneath, and the rail whips through hard right-angle cuts, drifts, and stacked S-curves as you and the rivals trade the lead. Cut the run into duels: each rival you close on breaks away into a fast sequence of turns before you finally take it.

Go bright, not dark. The floor is a bone-white lattice under a blown-out pale sky, hard-edged and clean, everything reading as black silhouette against light. The ribbons carry the only saturated color — yours warm gold, the rivals a cold electric violet — so the plain slowly fills with two colors of light. Score it with a hard sequenced pulse under wide, slow orchestral low strings: machine precision on top of something enormous and patient underneath, tightening with each duel.

Enemies come from the riders. A rival rider doesn't fight you directly at first — it calls down flights of fast aerial drones that peel off it in formation, so killing the rider is what stops the stream.

Finale: the last rival runs you into a gauntlet of shutter barriers dropping across the track, each one fed by a gate system off to the side. Shoot the gates in time and the barrier folds away as you reach it; miss the window and you take the wall. Clear the last gate and the rival has nowhere left to run — its ribbon collapses, the whole grid dims to bare white, level ends.
