# Crystal Corridor — theme prompt

_The theme for this level, in the form handed to an implementing agent alongside `docs/level-brief.md`._

---

The theme is a neon crystal corridor.

**The world.** You're flying down a winding tunnel deep inside some enormous crystal
formation. Everything is glowing wireframe on near-black — thin hot lines, no solid
surfaces — mostly cyan and magenta. The tunnel is receding wireframe ribs, with
faceted crystal clusters drifting past in the dark outside. Cold, clean, precise.

**Gameplay.** Around 45 seconds, in three acts. Act one is a warm-up: calm
formations of crystal shapes that hold still, drift, or orbit, so you learn the
rhythm. In act two the corridor wakes up: waves get denser and a nastier crystal
type appears that shoots glowing shard bolts back at you — they home in on your
view, and you have to shoot them down before they land. You can only take three
hits. Then a short breath of quiet, and act three is a boss: a huge crystal lattice
filling the tunnel ahead, with orbiting shield plates you break away in stages to
expose the core. Killing it is the finale.

**Music and sound.** Minor key, mid-120s BPM, arranged in the same three acts and
escalating with the action. Give each act its own instrument voices so the level
audibly changes character as it goes — locks tick upward in pitch as you paint
targets, and the boss act should toll, with shield breaks and core hits landing
like heavy bells.

**Details that sell it.** Locking a crystal flares it magenta. Kills shatter into
shard bursts. The boss entrance breaks the palette for a moment — flood the
corridor in hot gold so you feel something bigger has arrived.
