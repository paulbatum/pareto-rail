# Strandline

Fly inside the trailing curtain of a colossal jellyfish, banking between living green-gold tentacles while sickly violet parasites tear loose to defend their colony. A wide curve briefly reveals the bell as a green moon before the rail dives back through the strands and climbs to the infested crown.

## Visual language
Clear turquoise water falls to deep ocean blue with distance; thin sun shafts, suspended plankton, caustic arcs, translucent anatomy, and dozens of procedural tentacles carry jade, green-gold, and warm bioluminescence. Parasites alone use bruised black-violet shells and sour magenta organs. The crown parent sits behind three physical web sectors fed by pulsing broods, and the successful finish pulls far enough back to frame the clean animal for the first time.

## Musical language
128 BPM in D dorian, 32 bars and exactly 60 seconds. The score begins as slow submerged pads and a low body pulse, then restores glassy arpeggios, high plankton shimmer, and brighter harmonic layers as the jelly wakes. The crown introduces an abrasive parasite voice; locks, volleys, hits, and a written kill lane stay quantized to live harmony. Tearing the parent loose resolves into a sparse D-major bloom.

## Mechanical signature
A four-hull run opened by the six-lock command AWAKEN, with strand-clamped graspers that visibly tear loose, full-width manta skaters, two-stage corkscrewing nurse parasites, interceptable venom pods, three emerging brood sacs, and a six-lock staged parent. The parent is un-lockable while any brood feeds its web; every brood kill visibly removes one lattice sector, leaving the parent bare for the final volleys. RETURN arms the replay.
