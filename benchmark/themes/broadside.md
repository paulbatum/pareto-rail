# Broadside

Build a level where you're a strike fighter in the middle of a full fleet engagement - two lines of kilometer-long cruisers hammering each other while swarms of small craft dogfight in the space between. The rail is all momentum: hard banks and corkscrews through the crossfire, then long high-speed runs skimming the flank of a friendly cruiser close enough that its turrets and hull plating blur past, enemy fighters strafing it as you pick them off. Keep space bright, not black: the battle happens against a huge pale star low behind the enemy line, so ships read as silhouettes rimmed in white-gold, and the space between is laced with tracer streams, drifting wrecks, and slow-blooming explosions. Friendly fire is warm amber, enemy fire hard cyan-white, so the two sides read at a glance. Score it like a war film, not a club: brass and low strings, big and orchestral, swelling with each push and dropping to near silence in the eye of the battle.

Enemies are the swarm craft - fast, small, coming in waves off the enemy carriers.

Boss: the enemy flagship, in two phases. First a close-range pass along its hull, taking out its shield generators one by one while its point defense fills the space around you; the shield falls, escorting fighters pour in, and you fight through them as the rail comes around for the second pass; then a dive into its trenchwork to destroy the exposed power systems at its core. When the last one blows, the camera pulls out - past the breaking flagship, past both fleets, the whole battle in frame as the enemy line burns and scatters - won, level ends.
