# Strandline

Fly inside the trailing filaments of a gigantic jellyfish and cut away the violet colony feeding on it. A broad outward bank reveals the bell as a green moon before the route folds back through the living forest and climbs to the infested crown.

## Visual language
Clear blue-green water falls into deep cobalt distance beneath pale sun shafts. Forty-eight procedural tentacles carry green-gold pulses from the jellyfish bell; translucent membranes, pearl motes, and warm living nodes contrast with the parasites' bruised violet shells and sour magenta cores. RESTORE and RETURN are pearl-grid glyphs framed like small pieces of living membrane.

## Musical language
120 BPM heard in half-time: low membrane pulses and filtered water begin almost alone, then glassy overtones, current noise, a brighter sequence, and a melodic player lane accrete as the animal wakes. The crown borrows a sour semitone and a tighter pulse; tearing the parent loose ducks the colony rhythm into a serene high-D resolution.

## Mechanical signature
A 60-second, four-hull run with strand-clamped parasites that detach and hunt, broad ribbon defenders, orbiting spore schools, and a staged crown parent. Three two-lock brood-hearts physically remove three layers of webbing and expose the parent's six-lock carapace. A successful kill triggers a long camera retreat until the whole clean animal is finally visible.
