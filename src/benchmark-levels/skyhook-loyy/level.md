# Skyhook

A sixty-second defense of a climber car on its ride from rain to orbit. The cable stays beside the player for the entire ascent while storm cloud, blue sky, thin indigo, stars, and the planet's curved limb replace one another; at the top, a huge crawler descends the same tether until the station captures the car.

## Visual language
The sky supplies the color: storm slate, sunlit blue, indigo, and near-black. Everything built is blunt aerospace hardware—white enamel panels, graphite structure, small hazard-orange markings, exposed fasteners, and stamped maintenance glyphs. Rain, torn cloud banks, falling debris, the receding planet, and a camera-seated car deck sell vertical speed without neon.

## Musical language
At 96 BPM the lower climb is wide and atmospheric: filtered wind, suspended chords, cable harmonics, pressure pulses, and sheet-metal percussion. Each altitude band removes layers until vacuum is mostly isolated tether resonance. Locks follow the live chord, kills perform a hidden melodic lane, crawler damage strains the low register, and docking resolves into one long station chord before quiet.

## Mechanical signature
The six orange lamps on the cockpit sill are the climber car's integrity, while paired warning chevrons identify active car-divers. Wind kites and crossing skimmers occupy the weather; orbital raiders charge their signal eyes before throwing interceptable debris; two-hit saboteurs flare their grapples as they dive directly at the car. At bar 16 a massive tether crawler appears far overhead with three breakable clamps. Sever them to expose its two-stage core and destroy it before its uninterrupted descent reaches the car at bar 22.
